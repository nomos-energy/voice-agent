# Example call 4: a MaLo identification — the real, unscripted call

The three transcripts above are cleaned-up renditions of what a *good agent* sounds like. This one
is different: it is a **real Nomos clearing call, transcribed as it actually happened** and only
lightly anonymized. All names, identifiers and addresses have been replaced with synthetic ones,
but the disfluencies, the hold music, the dead air and the dead ends are left in on purpose — so
you can hear what the flow and tone of a real call are actually like.

It is a **MaLo-Ident** call (the same case type as `CASE-C` in `fixtures.json`, but a different,
messier instance — it is not driven by a fixture). Nomos is trying to find the market-location
number (the MaLo) for a connection it wants to register, because the automated lookup returned
nothing. Crucially, this call does **not** end with a clean answer — and that is exactly why it is
worth hearing. The clerk cannot find a MaLo for the meter, discovers the meter was removed, puts
the caller on hold, escalates the case to another internal department, and promises to come back
early next week. Real calls are often like this.

Two speakers:

- **[Speaker 0]** — the grid operator's clerk (*Sachbearbeiterin*).
- **[Speaker 1]** — the Nomos caller.

The recording came in two segments; the timestamps restart at the segment break, and a few lines
carry raw transcription noise (a merged speaker tag, a malformed timestamp) — left as-is.

**What to notice.** This is the messy reality the agent has to survive, not a clean win:

- **It is slow and disfluent.** Long pauses, hold music, "ähm", half-finished sentences, the clerk
  searching across several screens. An agent has to stay patient through dead air and a hold, and
  not talk over the clerk.
- **The number read-backs are the whole game.** Both sides spell the meter number out digit by
  digit and echo it back, and later the clerk reads the *Vorgangsnummer* in three chunks and the
  caller repeats each one. That digit-by-digit discipline is exactly what your agent must do.
- **The outcome is inconclusive, and that is realistic.** There is no MaLo assigned to the meter;
  the clerk finds a meter removal (*Ausbau*, dated 29 January) and a *Siedlungsanlage*, the case is
  already partly in progress (a *Zuarbeit* to one department), and a new *Zuarbeit* to another
  department is needed. The call ends with a handed-over *Vorgangsnummer* and a promise of an
  interim update early next week.
- **The right "next step" here is to log it and wait.** A good agent captures the *Vorgangsnummer*
  correctly, records that the grid operator will come back, and triggers the follow-up — it does
  **not** bluff a resolution or a clean MaLo it never got.
